Is There A Link Between Access to Healthcare and Poverty?

Correlations Discovered Between Healthcare and Poverty Rates

There is a strong correlation between poverty rate and a lack of access to healthcare in the United States. Mississippi, Louisiana, Texas and Georgia have the highest poverty rates in the 18 - 21% range while also having high rates of lack of healthcare in the range of 18 - 20%. Generally, a lower poverty rate is associated with a lower lack of healthcare rate, demonstrated by a large cluster formed by Massachusetts, Hawaii, Vermont, Maryland, Connecticut, Iowa, Minnesota and North Dakota which have poverty rates closer to 12% and below and lack of healthcare rates below 10%.
